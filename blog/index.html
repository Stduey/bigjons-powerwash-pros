How Often Should You Power Wash Your Driveway in Southern California?

Big Jon's PowerWash Pros — San Dimas, CA

5 min read — Spring 2026

If you live in San Dimas, the San Gabriel Valley, or the Greater Los Angeles area, your driveway takes a beating all year long. Dust, traffic grime, sprinkler overspray, tree debris, mildew, and the occasional oil stain can build up faster than most homeowners expect. One of the most common questions people ask is: how often should you power wash your driveway in Southern California?

For most homes, a good rule of thumb is once or twice a year. In Southern California, driveways often collect a mix of dirt, tire residue, leaves, hard-water staining, and sun-baked buildup. If your home gets a lot of foot traffic, sits under mature trees, or sees frequent vehicle use, you may benefit from more frequent cleaning.

In areas like San Dimas and the surrounding San Gabriel Valley, curb appeal matters. A stained driveway can make the whole front of a property look older or poorly maintained, even when the rest of the home is in good shape. Regular power washing helps restore a cleaner appearance and can also reduce slippery buildup caused by mildew, algae, or damp shaded areas.

Seasonally, spring and early summer are great times to schedule service. Many homeowners in the Greater LA area use spring cleaning as a chance to freshen up patios, entryways, and driveways before the hottest months arrive. It also makes sense to clean after windy periods, landscaping work, or heavy outdoor entertaining when dirt and stains tend to accumulate.

For commercial properties, apartment walkways, common areas, restaurant exteriors, and warehouse surfaces, cleaning frequency may need to be much higher. Businesses in Southern California often benefit from routine exterior surface cleaning because appearance, safety, and first impressions all matter. Clean concrete and hard surfaces can help present a more professional image to customers, tenants, and visitors.

A lot of property owners consider renting a machine and doing the work themselves. While that can be tempting, not all surfaces should be treated the same way. Using the wrong pressure, nozzle, or cleaning method can damage concrete edges, surrounding surfaces, or nearby fixtures. Professional service is often the safer choice when you want a more even, thorough result.

Another reason not to wait too long is that older stains tend to become harder to remove. Dirt, grease, and organic buildup can settle deep into the surface over time. A maintenance schedule is usually more cost-effective than waiting until a driveway looks severely neglected.

If your driveway in San Dimas, the San Gabriel Valley, or the Greater Los Angeles area is starting to look dull, stained, or weathered, a professional wash can make a noticeable difference.
